# 🚴 Adventure Works Sales Dashboard | Power BI

## Short Description
A Power BI dashboard analyzing **$29.36M in sales** and **60K orders** for Adventure Works Bike Shop — covering country-wise performance, category profitability, seasonal trends, and production cost efficiency across 2010–2014.

## 📂 Data Source
- **Platform:** [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/) / Microsoft Sample Dataset
- **Scope:** Adventure Works bike shop sales data (2010–2014) covering countries, product categories, quarters, and production costs

---

## ✨ Key Findings & Highlights

### 📊 Sales Overview
- Total Sales: **$29.36M** | Profit: **$12.08M** | Orders: **60K** | Profit Margin: **41.15%**
- **2013 is the peak year at $16.35M** — more than **double 2012 ($5.84M)**, showing explosive growth
- **2014 drops to just $0.05M** — a near-complete revenue collapse that needs urgent investigation
- **December is the strongest month at $3.2M** vs February being the weakest at **$1.7M**
- **Q3 leads quarterly sales at $7.64M** and Q4 follows at $9.11M — second half of the year consistently outperforms

### 🌍 Country & Category Performance
- **USA leads with $9.39M in sales** but Australia is close behind at **$9.06M** — two markets driving ~62% of total revenue
- **Bikes dominate at $28.32M (96% of total sales)** with $11.51M profit — Accessories and Clothing are negligible contributors
- **Canada is the weakest market at $1.98M** — nearly **5x behind the US** despite being a neighboring market

### 🏭 Production Cost Insight
- **2013 production cost was $9.6M against $16.35M sales** — healthy margin, but cost scaled rapidly with growth
- **2011 had the best cost efficiency** — $4.2M production cost against $7.08M sales, delivering strong margins

---

## 💡 Recommendations
- **Investigate the 2014 revenue collapse** — dropping from $16.35M to $0.05M in one year is a critical red flag; data completeness or a major business disruption needs to be examined
- **Diversify beyond Bikes** — 96% revenue dependency on one category is a business risk; Accessories and Clothing need dedicated sales and marketing push
- **Double down on USA and Australia** — both markets are nearly equal in size; increasing marketing investment in these two could significantly grow overall revenue
- **Target February and Q1 slump** — sales dip to $1.7M in February; seasonal promotions or bundled offers could help smooth the revenue curve
